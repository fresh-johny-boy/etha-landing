# ETHA Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
**Joana.** Exhausted, skeptical, scrolling between meetings. Arrives from Instagram or Facebook ads — always on mobile (390px). She's tried wellness things before and felt nothing. She wants to feel seen, not sold to. She has 5 seconds before she scrolls past. The job: make her pause, feel recognised, take the quiz.

**Context:** Phone, probably mid-afternoon, between tasks. One thumb. Low patience for anything that feels curated or try-hard.

### Brand Personality
**"Playful Authority."** Three words: **ancient, alive, unpolished.** ETHA doesn't shout. It whispers with total conviction. The brand knows something Joana hasn't remembered yet — and it waits for her to catch up.

The tension that defines everything: ancient wisdom expressed through a completely contemporary visual language. Not spiritual kitsch. Not sterile wellness tech. Something that feels like it was always there.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Light sections** (`#FFEFDE` cream) alternate with **dark sections** (`#3D233B` aubergine). This is not a light/dark toggle — it's a deliberate tonal rhythm. Every colour transition uses organic SVG wave dividers, never a hard edge.

**Typography:** Plantin (serif) carries ALL emotional weight — headlines, body, storytelling. Brandon Grotesque is UPPERCASE ONLY for labels, navigation, metadata — it is wayfinding, not voice. Zero letter-spacing on Plantin. Wide tracking (0.2em+) on Brandon always.

**The Aura:** Organic SVG stroke paths — the brand's core visual identity. Three states: Balanced (gentle oval), Relaxed (flowing blob with loops), Energised (angular, self-crossing). Always `fill: none`, continuous, cubic beziers only. Never geometric. The aura communicates energy state, it is not decoration.

**0px border-radius everywhere.** Organic quality comes from Aura SVGs and nature imagery, not rounded UI chrome.

**Motion:** GSAP + Lenis. Slow but immense. Forces, not animation. Parallax depth, long loops, gravity. One well-orchestrated scroll reveal per section beats scattered micro-interactions.

### Anti-References
Explicitly forbidden aesthetic directions:

- **Clinical perfection** — forms with no imperfections, over-structured grids, anything that reads as SaaS or health-tech
- **Instagram wellness** — posed yoga shots, strong filters, over-saturated images, flat lays, curated lifestyle
- **Cosy/decorative** — warm-neutral Kinfolk aesthetic, linen textures, candle vibes, anything twee
- **Generic wellness purple** — purple-to-blue gradients on white, glow effects, glassmorphism
- **Transformation narrative** — before/after framing, "unlock your potential," any language that implies Joana is broken

**Image direction (from brand guide):**
- DO: Nature in motion with graphic shapes and flowing lines. People living in the moment. Ingredients in close-up, highlighting texture and natural forms. Product with part of the human body, calm and simple.
- DON'T: Stereotyped nature that appears worthy or photoshopped. Instagram-style posed shots. Clinical studio shots with no imperfections. Flat lays. Graphic studio shoots with no movement.

### Design Principles
1. **Organic over geometric** — "There are no straight lines or 90-degree angles in nature." Every hard edge is a brand violation.
2. **Motion as metaphor** — Aura animations communicate energy state (balanced/relaxed/energised). Movement should feel like forces, not animation.
3. **Restraint with conviction** — Few elements. Never crowded. Never ornamental. Generous whitespace is not empty — it is the brand breathing.
4. **Imperfection is intentional** — "Perfectly Imperfect." Asymmetric layouts, offset headlines, organic irregularity. Symmetric centered layouts are wrong.
5. **One system** — Landing page and quiz share the same design language. The quiz is not a separate product; it is the emotional continuation of the landing page journey.

### Scope
Both landing page and quiz flow use this single design system. The quiz has interactive constraints (step-by-step, no long scroll) but must feel like the same brand — same type, same colours, same aura states, same motion language. Transitioning from landing → quiz should feel like walking deeper into the same world, not switching apps.

### Color Quick Reference
- Background light: `#FFEFDE` (Cream)
- Background dark: `#3D233B` (Aubergine)
- Text on light: `#3D233B`
- Text on dark: `#FFEFDE`
- Vata: light `#86C4FB` / dark `#000F9F` / accent `#F5A800`
- Pitta: light `#FF5C3A` / dark `#9D0033` / accent `#A2E8F2`
- Kapha: light `#6BCDB2` / dark `#00545C` / accent `#FFB3A5`
- Antidote colours (Citrine/Aquamarine/Carnelian): decorative aura lines only, never text
- Shadows: always aubergine-tinted `rgba(61,35,59,...)`

### Stack
Next.js 16 + React 19 + TypeScript + Tailwind CSS v4 (`@theme inline`) + GSAP + ScrollTrigger + Lenis. Fonts self-hosted via `next/font/local`. All brand tokens in `app/src/app/globals.css`.
