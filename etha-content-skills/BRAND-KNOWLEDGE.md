ETHA stands for **ether (ākāśa)** — the space that holds everything, the source of transformation, and the invitation to return to self.

- A brand where Ayurvedic wisdom meets modern sensory science
- A space of ritual, not routine
- A company built on plant intelligence and poetic clarity
- A movement back to natural rhythm, glow, and grace
- A brand for Joana, not for the masses

## Brand Essence

ETHA is not a beauty brand. ETHA is a **ritual system**.

We sell rhythm, presence, and sensory awakening, guided by Ayurvedic wisdom and modern science. ETHA is rooted in ether (ākāśa) — space, stillness, and the source of energy. Every formula, word, and visual must return Joana to herself. We don't chase trends. We guide her home to balance.

## Brand Characteristics

### Radical Traditionalists
Born of indignation towards the disconnected modern lifestyle, ETHA blends the best of the ancient with the best of the contemporary.

- **DO:** Set out an inspiring, emotive vision. Have roots in ancient tradition but feel fresh and inventive. Lead by example.
- **DON'T:** Focus solely on rational product benefits. Follow fads or competitors. Talk the talk without walking the walk.

### Perfectly Imperfect
There are no straight lines in nature, so we celebrate the imperfect. We are not dogmatic about Ayurveda — even the devout stray from the path, and that's ok.

- **DO:** Celebrate every step of the journey. Be imaginative, optimistic, liberating. Embrace natural inconsistencies.
- **DON'T:** Make people feel guilty for not being totally committed. Be preachy or "holier than thou." Eradicate imperfection through cold, clinical design.

### Playful Authority
We take what we do seriously, but not ourselves. Like a yogi, our work is easiest when we balance practice with play.

- **DO:** Act like a warm, welcoming, wise friend. Turn complex ideas into easy, imaginative experiences. Inspire and delight.
- **DON'T:** Be a strict school teacher or be silly and superficial. Complicate, confuse, or bore with jargon. Guilt trip or hard sell.

# Brand Voice & Tone

## Tone Checklist

- Poetic, but never fluffy
- Sacred, but never new-age vague
- Clean, but never sterile
- Empowering, but never pushy
- Warm, intelligent, beautiful

> "If it could sit next to a pharmacy product, it's not ETHA. If Joana would whisper 'wow' when reading it — it's ETHA."

## Copy House Rules

- Use conversational contractions: "can't" not "cannot," "don't" not "do not"
- **Avoid exclamation marks** — energy is conveyed through language, not punctuation
- When speaking of Doshas: "Vata is" or "Vata has" — never "Vatas are" or "a Vata has"
- Always capitalize: Ayurveda, Dosha, Vata, Pitta, Kapha
- Don't use ampersands — always spell out "and"
- Brand name in body copy: "Etha" (not all-caps ETHA, unless in logo context)
- Use Indian and Ayurvedic terms to build brand vernacular and intrigue
- Focus on Joana's experience, not ETHA's features — a true guru focuses on the pupil
- Describe products holistically: aroma, sensation, energy, ritual context — never just function
- Use visual metaphors and onomatopoeia to evoke nature: "Make waves," "Sea spray on sun-parched skin"

## Two Content Moods

Content adapts its **mood** based on whether the product/moment is relaxing or energizing, while keeping voice consistent:

| Mood | Writing Style | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Relaxing** | Softer, adjective-heavy, longer flowing sentences. Slow cadence. | Moonlight Hug, evening rituals, calming products |
| **Energising** | Active verbs, enthusiasm, punchy sentence structure. Quick cadence. | Sunrise Bliss, morning rituals, activating products |

## Product Naming Convention

Product names use **evocative natural metaphors** that highlight the elemental properties of ingredients in relation to the Dosha. Examples:
- "After the Rain Monsoon Mask" (Vata)
- "Blue Beam Cooling Corporeal Balm" (Pitta)
- "Undercurrent 26,000 ft Mask" (Kapha)

When ingredients are recognizable in English (turmeric, ginger, rose water), use English names. For all others, use the Indian/Sanskrit word.

# Content Guidelines

## DO:
- Center everything around the ritual moment (Morning: clarity, warmth, activation. Evening: grounding, release, stillness.)
- Speak in poetic, grounded language. Think: "Let your skin drink," "Hydration that anchors," "Glow like still water"
- Elevate the hero herb as the guide of the formula. Every ETHA product has one botanical protagonist with a story, origin, texture, energy. Example: "Moringa, slow-pressed in Tamil Nadu, hydrates like dew on parched earth"
- Design for slowness, beauty, and reverence. ETHA is a minimalist, luxury apothecary experience. Leave white space. Use soft tones. Invite the eye inward.
- Reinforce Ayurvedic science + modern functionality. Mention Dosha. Reference rituals. Cite bioavailability or fermentation if relevant — but keep it lyrical.

- Do not copy or mimic other wellness brands. ETHA is not another clean beauty brand. We are not Goop. Not Moon Juice. Not Tata Harper. We are ETHA.
- Do not use beauty cliches or mass-market tropes. No "your best skin ever," "magic," "game changer," "clean girl," or "boosted glow." Speak like a modern Ayurvedic sage, not an Instagram ad.
- Do not write to the market. Write to Joana. Speak intimately, intelligently, and spiritually. Never use generic tone. Always use the language of presence.
- Do not list ingredients without their story or purpose. Every plant must feel alive, ancestral, and necessary. Example: "Shatavari — cooling root of feminine resilience, gathered at moonrise"
- Do not lose the polarity of morning and evening. Morning = clarity, lightness, focus, expansion. Evening = release, restoration, stillness, softness.

## All Creative Outputs Must:
1. Lead with ritual and moment of use
2. Center on 1 hero herb
3. Speak to Joana's emotional transformation
4. Be visually and linguistically sensual
5. Embed Ayurvedic truth without jargon
6. Always return to Earth, breath, rhythm, beauty

> If your words, visuals, or formulas do not slow Joana down, guide her inward, or reflect the land and lineage — go back. This is sacred. Treat it that way.

## Content Structure Templates (by Type)

### Product Description
1. Open with a **feeling or texture** — not the product name or benefit
2. Describe the **hero herb like a person** (personality, origin, energy)
3. Tie the product to a **Dosha moment** (e.g., "Vata at 7am")
4. Mention **texture, scent, and sensory transformation** during use
5. Describe **emotional outcomes**, not clinical benefits
6. End with a line that feels like a **quiet exhale**

### Dosha Quiz Result
1. Frame it as an **invitation, not a judgment**
2. Describe the Dosha's energy using **movement, season, breath**
3. Speak to Joana as if you **know her well**
4. Offer a **starting ritual**, a **suggested herb**, and a **mantra**
5. End by reminding her: she is not broken — she is returning

### Email (Nurture / Post-Quiz)
1. Begin with **how she might be feeling** right now
2. Use **one metaphor, one plant, one pause**
3. Include a subtle suggestion of what to try or read next — **not a push**
4. Close with a **ritual phrase or affirmation**, not a CTA button

### Social Media Caption
1. Start with **rhythm** (e.g., "Morning. Skin. Stillness.")
2. Speak to a **moment Joana would recognize**
3. Avoid explanation — **offer mood**
4. Include one **sensory hook** (a scent, a texture, a shift)
5. End with a **soft ritual invitation** (e.g., "Take this with tea. Before the emails.")

### Web Copy (Homepage / About / Quiz Landing)
1. It must feel like an **arrival**
2. Use short, **breath-length sentences**
3. Begin with a reframe: "You are not [X]. You are [Y]."
4. Mention **rhythm, ritual, or remembering**
5. No buzzwords, no mission statements — make it **human**

# Industry Trends (2025-2026) — Content Themes

> Source: Mintel 2026 Global Beauty & Personal Care Predictions. Only trends that align with ETHA's positioning are included.

## 1. Metabolic Beauty (Inside-Out Wellness)

Consumers are moving beyond surface-level aesthetics toward measurable, inside-out wellbeing. Skin is becoming the body's "dashboard." Beauty is entering the preventative health arena — proof-driven, personalized, and preventative.

**Why it matters for ETHA content:** Validates ETHA's supplements + Dosha diagnostic positioning. Content can reference the shift from "beauty as cosmetics" to "beauty as inner balance" — which is exactly what ETHA already does with Ayurveda.

**Content angles:**
- Ayurveda was doing "inside-out beauty" for 5,000 years before it became a trend
- Your Dosha is the original personalized wellness diagnostic
- Supplements as ritual, not pills — ETHA's adaptogenic stacks align inner energy with outer radiance

## 2. Sensorial Synergy (Experience-First Beauty)

Consumers seek products that make them feel as good as they look. Beauty is becoming less about outcomes and more about experiences. Multi-sensory (scent, texture, touch) and emotional regulation are driving purchase decisions. By 2030, beauty will be judged on its ability to regulate emotions, not just deliver results.

**Why it matters for ETHA content:** This IS ETHA's core — ritual, sensory transformation, texture evolution, scent journeys. ETHA was built for this moment.

**Content angles:**
- Ritual over routine — ETHA's Sunrise Bliss and Moonlight Hug are sensory experiences, not product applications
- Texture as transformation — oil-to-velvet, cooling masks, scent-evolving creams
- 37% of Ayurvedic skincare buyers pay more for sensory effects (cooling/warming)
- Beauty as emotional regulation: "held, bright, grounded, open, fluid"

## 3. Human Touch Revolution (Anti-Algorithm Authenticity)

Consumers are exhausted by AI-driven perfection. 70% of under-45s say AI makes them value human-made things more. Raw authenticity, artisanal craft, and imperfection are becoming luxury signals. "Hand to face" may redefine beauty the way "farm to table" redefined food.

**Why it matters for ETHA content:** ETHA's voice is already sacred, grounded, and human. Content should lean into wildcrafted sourcing, hand-harvested herbs, the maker's story. Never sound AI-generated.

**Content angles:**
- Wildcrafted, slow-infused, copper-distilled — every ingredient has a human story
- ETHA's formulas are crafted, not manufactured
- Behind the scenes of sourcing and ritual design
- Imperfection as beauty — Ayurveda sees beauty as balance, not perfection

## 4. Additional Micro-Trends (GWI 2025)

**Mind-Skin Connection (Psycho-Dermatology):** Growing recognition that emotional states directly impact skin health. Stress, anxiety, and sleep quality manifest visibly on skin. Content angle: "Your skin feels what you feel — ETHA rituals calm both."

**Hormonal Wellness for Women:** Surge in demand for personalized hormonal support — ties directly to Shatavari (hormonal balance) and ETHA's Dosha-based approach. Content angle: "Your cycle is a rhythm. ETHA helps you flow with it, not fight it."

**Sleep as Wellness Pillar:** Sleep quality is now a primary wellness concern. Ties directly to Moonlight Hug evening ritual. Content angle: "Moonlight Hug isn't skincare before bed — it's the ritual that lets you surrender to rest."
