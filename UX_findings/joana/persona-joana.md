# Joana — Walkthrough

Tested on 390×844 mobile emulation. Joana voice: honest, skeptical, early-30s overstimulated. She has ~5 seconds on entry and no insider knowledge of ETHA.

## Blind First Read

> *Instagram ad → lands on /quiz. "Am I who I am supposed to be?" — big serif, cream on aubergine. Okay, that's a real question. Not "unlock your potential." I pause. Then a CONTINUE button and "intro 0/6."*

> *Six? I'm already doing six screens before the quiz? I tap once. "If this question has ever moved through you, even once, even briefly, then you already know there is more to you than what the world sees." Okay that's a lot of clauses. I tap again. "There is a system, older than medicine, older than modern science…" — I'm skimming now. Tap. "Your body has a blueprint." Tap. "YOU ARE ABOUT TO RECEIVE: Your personal rhythm map…" — okay this is a value ladder. Why was it buried behind 5 screens?*

> *"Are you ready to return to yourself?" Hmm. Close to the wellness-account-I-muted energy. But the BEGIN YOUR REMEMBERING button is Plantin, not some gradient button. I tap it because I've committed this much already.*

> *First real question: "Think about the first moments of waking. The first breath of morning. What wakes with you?" — My mind / My will / The bed. Oh — that's specific. "The bed" made me laugh, that's my answer. I tap it. No feedback, the screen changes.*

> *I'm on question 1 of 52. FIFTY-TWO. And I can see three "layers" at the top — BODY, MIND, SPIRIT. So this is long.*

> *I answer three more. They're good — concrete, physical, not abstract. Then I look up and realise I don't know how far I've come. I tap out, check Instagram, come back, hit refresh by accident. Now I'm at question 1 again. All my answers are gone. I close the tab.*

## Think

- Ayurveda is a 5,000-year-old body-map system. (Useful frame — I didn't know that in these terms.)
- There are 3 layers — body, mind, spirit. Makes sense for a holistic thing.
- I still don't know what "Dosha," "Vata," "Pitta," or "Kapha" mean. I assume I'll find out.
- I don't know how long the quiz actually takes. 52 questions at ~5 seconds each = 4+ minutes. Too long for "between meetings."
- The intro's promise ("rhythm map, morning-to-night ritual, botanical recommendations") is more concrete than I expected. Good.

## Feel

- **Screen 1:** intrigued. The hook is actually a real question, not hype.
- **Screens 2–5:** patience draining. Too much prose before the thing starts.
- **Screen 6:** on the edge of "this is every wellness brand." The word "remembering" is doing too much work.
- **First question:** re-engaged. "The bed" answer unlocked a laugh. Good.
- **Question 4–5:** curious, enjoying the specificity of the questions.
- **Counter seen (1/52):** mild panic. How long is this?
- **Accidental refresh → reset:** betrayal. I spent 90 seconds on the intro and 4 questions, now gone. I will not redo this today.

## Do

On a real phone, real context: I close after the refresh. No saved progress, no resume prompt = funnel death.

If I survived to the email gate: I give the email. The result page copy (if it reads like the one I saw) would earn the hand-over. The tonight-ritual lands — three breaths on my back, hands on belly. Specific. Doable. I'd try it tonight.

I'd probably NOT open the follow-up emails tomorrow unless the first email referenced the specific thing I said in the quiz.
