But first, what is Ayurveda?   Ayurveda is an ancient system of Indian healthcare, steadily
                               developed and refined over the last 5,000 years. Its name
                               means 'Science of Life.'
                               Taking a holistic and personalised approach to health,
                               Ayurveda increases your vitality through recommending
                               the right balance of diet, exercise, massage, yoga and herbal
                               remedies for each body type – which are known as Doshas.
                               More about those in a minute.

                                        Ayurveda is huge in the East, but little known in
                                        the West. So, Etha’s goal is to create a global brand
                                        that disrupts the superficial, corporate health &
                                        wellbeing market by making this ancient knowledge
                                        modern, accessible & relevant to today’s world -
                                        so that everyone can benefit from it.

So what are Doshas?   Ayurveda reminds us that everything in life is nature – and
                      everything in nature is made up of five elements: earth, fire,
                      water, air and space (also known as ether).
                      Since us humans are part of nature too, our bodies are also
                      made up of these elements – in three different blends, which
                      Ayurveda calls Doshas.
                               Your precise Dosha balance is as unique as you
                               are, but for each of us, one Dosha tends to be more
                               dominant and underpin the lifestyle that suits us
                               best – as each is associated with a set of physical,
                               emotional and mental qualities.
                               Etha's product architecture is based around
                               this Dosha system, allowing you to curate your
                               own palette of products tailored to your body’s
                               unique needs.

The Doshas       The Doshas are called Vata, Pitta and Kapha.
                 See which you can recognise in yourself.

                         Vata:
                         Ruled by air and ether, Vata changes fast and often, like the
                         wind. It’s unpredictable, talkative, impulsive and creative.
                         Physically, Vata has a slender frame and quick movements,
                         with chilly hands and feet.

                         Pitta:
                         Fuelled by two opposite elements – water and fire – Pitta’s
                         inner heat is a force of nature. Switched-on and intense, it has
                         a passionate spirit and intellectual mind. Physically, Pitta has
                         an athletic build with flexible joints.

                         Kapha:
                         Governed by earth and water, Kapha
                         is a steady and grounded Dosha. Patient and reliable, with
                         a kind and generous nature Kapha has a strong physical build
                         with round features, thick hair and smooth skin that doesn’t
                         wrinkle easily.

Our name (pronounced ee-ther ) derives from
‘ether’, the first element and energy source that
affects change on all things in the natural world.
Its Sanskrit counterpart ‘akasha’ means to
radiate — to convey energy without resistance.
In western terms it has instant connotations
with the elements and the mystical realm that
lies ‘over the rainbow’…

Given a modern twist in which the ‘er’ is
substituted for an ‘a’, the word becomes
pleasingly balanced and symmetrical, with an
open end. Etha is intrinsically tied to Ayurveda
as well as having relevant English meaning.

TONE OF VOICE
I N TRODUCTION




                 Just like visual identity, tone of voice is integral to a brand’s
                 personality. A strong tone of voice is compelling and emotive,
                 and the target consumer should see a little of themselves in
                 it. It should convey the brand’s values, mission and attitudes
                 without directly stating them.
                          For a brand to be authentic, the tone of voice must
                          run consistently through all copy, from the brand
                          strap-line to the legal small-print. This level of detail
                          is the difference between a consumer buying into
                          a brand and its beliefs or not – something that’s
                          especially important for a brand with a strong story
                          and a diverse product range.

H OUSE RULES




Here are some house       Although the logo is in all-caps, the brand name should
                          always appear like this in body copy: Etha.
rules to consider when
                          Use conversational contractions: can’t instead of cannot, don’t
creating copy for Etha.   instead of do not, etc.

                          We describe ourselves in first person as an organisation, so
                          we say “We have” or “Etha has” rather than “They have” or
                          “Etha have”.

                          Avoid exclamation marks. Energy can be more powerfully
                          conveyed through language rather than punctuation.

                          When speaking of the Doshas, we say “Vata is” or “Vata has”
                          rather than “Vatas are” or “A Vata has”.

                          The words Ayurveda, Dosha, Vata, Pitta and Kapha should
                          always be capitalised.

                          Don’t use ampersands. Always spell out the word ‘and’, even
                          on social media.
