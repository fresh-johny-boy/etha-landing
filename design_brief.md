## Slide 2

What Etha  Is Etha is a modern  interpreter  of ancient  authority . It behaves like a  5,000-year-old Ayurvedic guru reborn as a  calm, playful, contemporary guide —never preachy, never corporate, never mystical-for-mysticism’s-sake. Core controversaries that  define everything: Ancient × Modern Science × Spirituality Authority × Warmth Structure × Imperfection This tension is  intentional  and must always be visible.

---

## Slide 3

Why Etha looks the way it looks Etha’s visual identity is  not “pretty wellness branding.” It is a  controlled expression of natural energy . Visual DNA Organic, imperfect forms  (no hard geometry, no symmetry obsession) Motion over stillness  (nothing feels static, frozen, or posed) Nature as force, not decoration Restraint : few elements, never crowded, never ornamental Everything should feel: Alive In motion Temporarily captured This is why: Flat lays are avoided Studio perfection is rejected Over-designed layouts feel  wrong

---

## Slide 4

The Aura (Most important visual asset) The Aura is  not decoration!  It is the  visual metaphor for energy states . There are  three valid emotional modes : Balanced  → default, calm, centred Relaxed  → slow, rounded, soft Energised  → playful, angular, restless There are also 3 dosha states of the Aura  Rules that matter: It must always be a  continuous loop It must feel  intentional and restrained It should never compete with copy  and  logo at the same time It communicates  state , not style If the Aura is misused, the brand instantly feels amateur or decorative.

---

## Slide 6

Colour Logic (a navigation system) Colour is  functional , not expressive. Masterbrand =  Cream + Aubergine Cream → openness, breath, space Aubergine → authority, grounding, seriousness Dosha colours are: Identity Wayfinding Balance mechanisms Rules are strict for a reason: You never mix Dosha colours freely Antidote colours are used  intentionally Type is always restrained (never playful with colour) This is what keeps the brand from slipping into “wellness chaos.”

---

## Slide 8

Typography (Voice in visual form) Typography mirrors the brand’s philosophy: Plantin (serif) →  history, wisdom , literature, permanence Brandon Grotesque (sans) →  clarity, modernity , functionality The discipline matters: Hierarchy is sacred Spacing is generous Alignment is deliberate Nothing feels rushed or loud Typography should feel like  calm authority that doesn’t need to shout .

---

## Slide 10

Tone of Voice (where usually fail) Etha speaks like: A  wise friend who knows more than you, but never reminds you of it. Key traits: Warm Grounded Inviting Confident Never salesy Never preachy Never ironic or gimmicky Language principles: Sensory, visceral, nature-anchored Metaphor > explanation Invitation > instruction “We” > “You should” If the copy sounds clever, trendy, or marketing-driven — it’s wrong.

---

## Slide 12

Art Direction (Non-negotiables) Etha imagery must: Capture  moments , not poses Show  energy , not beauty ideals Feel  candid , not styled Avoid wellness clichés completely Nature is: Powerful Chaotic Calm Sometimes uncomfortable People are: In motion Diverse Present, not performing

HOMEPAGE DESIGNER BRIEF Etha is not designed. It is revealed. Nature leads. Humanity follows. Beauty emerges from truth, not decoration.

## Slide 16

Etha’s Moodboard + what the customer shou ld  feel 1. HERO —  Return to the Source Core feeling- Overwhelming Nature. Belonging. Awe. What the user should feel “I have come home — to where I belong. To something bigger than me, and deeply me.” This is not calm minimalism. This is  humbling, grounding, powerful nature  — the kind that makes you feel small  and  safe. Design direction Nature must dominate  the frame. Vast landscapes, raw elements, scale. Water that moves endlessly. Wind that bends grass or fabric. Light that feels ancient. Human presence is secondary : If present, it should feel absorbed into nature, not posing within it. Motion : Slow but immense (parallax depth, long loops, gravity). Movement should feel like forces, not animation. Typography : Bold simplicity. Large serif headline that feels  carved , not decorative. CTA : Feels like a threshold, not a button. Entering, crossing, returning. Still strong enough to make the user immediately want to client on the dosha test being the KEY CTA of the page Avoid Polished wellness imagery Lifestyle shots that feel curated Anything “cosy” or decorative at this stage This is  Nature as Origin .

## Slide 22

Etha’s Moodboard + what the customer shou ld  feel 2. THE FIVE ELEMENTS —  Awe & Realisation Core feeling - Wow. Respect. Reverence for Nature’s power. What the user should feel “I  did’t  fully grasp how powerful nature is — and  yet it lives both outside me and inside me.” This section should feel  striking , almost cinematic. ## Slide 27

Etha’s Moodboard + what the customer shou ld  feel 3. DOSHAS — Meaning & Recognition Core feeling- Understanding. Beauty with logic. What the user should feel “I see how these forces combine — and I recognise myself in the result.” This is where  aesthetics meet structure . Design direction Clear visual logic : Show how the  5 elements combine into 3 Doshas . This should be visible, not just explained. The user should understand what “being a certain dosha type” means Use of Dosha colour legend : Subtle but intentional. Gradients, halos, aura effects. Aura depiction – if used : Doshas feel alive, moving, breathing. Visual flow : Elements → Doshas → Self – unique dosha constitution. Motion encouraged Aesthetics : Refined, poetic, editorial-level beauty. Avoid Tables that feel instructional Overloaded copy Anything that feels diagnostic Recognition should feel  gentle, dignified, and beautiful .
